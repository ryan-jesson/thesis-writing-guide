8. Righting the Writing

Balancing Detail and Clarity

One of the trickiest aspects of writing methods and results is striking the right balance between providing enough detail and maintaining clarity. A helpful rule of thumb, especially for first drafts, is to err on the side of including too much detail rather than too little. This allows your supervisor to easily remove redundant or unnecessary content, whereas missing information is much harder to detect and correct.

Interactive Practice: Tense and Active Voice

In the methods section, use past tense consistently. This isn't just a stylistic choice â€” it accurately reflects that the study has already occurred. Moreover, favour an active voice over passive constructions. Instead of writing "Confidence was measured using a 5-point Likert scale," opt for "We measured participants' confidence using a 5-point Likert scale." This approach not only makes your writing more engaging but also clearly attributes actions to the researchers.

Try out the following interactive examples to practice identifying and revising sentences to use past tense and active voice appropriately:

Using Past Tense

Example 1:

Incorrect: Participants complete a series of spatial reasoning tasks and then answer questions about their strategy use.

Corrected: Participants completed a series of spatial reasoning tasks and then answered questions about their strategy use.

Example 2:

Incorrect: The experiment consists of three phases: training, testing, and debriefing. Each phase takes approximately 20 minutes.

Corrected: The experiment consisted of three phases: training, testing, and debriefing. Each phase took approximately 20 minutes.

Example 3:

Incorrect: We randomly assigned participants to conditions, but counterbalancing is used to control for order effects.

Corrected: We randomly assigned participants to conditions, but counterbalancing was used to control for order effects.

Using Active Voice

Example 1:

Incorrect: Response times were measured using a digital timer and accuracy was recorded by the experimenter.

Corrected: We measured response times using a digital timer and the experimenter recorded accuracy.

Example 2:

Incorrect: The questionnaires were administered in a randomized order and demographic information was collected at the end of the session.

Corrected: We administered the questionnaires in a randomized order and collected demographic information at the end of the session.

Example 3:

Incorrect: Trials were excluded from analysis if the reaction time was greater than three standard deviations from the mean.

Corrected: We excluded trials from analysis if the reaction time was greater than three standard deviations from the mean.
